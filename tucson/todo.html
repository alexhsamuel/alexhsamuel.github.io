Alex's Tucson

  • Caves

    The geological excitement continues underground. Big nearby sites include Kartchner Caverns State Park and Colossal Cave.

    I love caves but haven't been to these yet.

  • Tombstone, AZ

    A real Wild West mining boom town and site of the "Shootout at the O.K. Corral". Now exists as a Disneyfied but still fairly amusing characature of itself.

    It's fun if you are open to some cheesiness. I haven't seen most of the sights and shows.

  • Bisbee

    A real mining town nestled among steep hills; feels a bit like a wilder mini-San Fran. Saloons, magic shows, and of course gift shops. Huge open-pit mines, some in use until the late 20th century, and mine shaft tours.

    Definitely cute and more real than Tombstone.

  • Airplane Boneyard

  • Pima Air & Space Museum

  • Titan Missle Museum

    There are also several other sealed-up Titan missle sites scattered around the area.

  • Valley of the Sun

    Never been, even though it's only a short way from home.

  • Kitt Peak

    This place is unbelievably cool and completely worth freezing your ass in the cold mountain air.

  • Biosphere 2

    Haven't been there for many, many years.

  • San Xavier del Bac

    Kinda cool, but seems shoddy if you've been to the nicer churches in Mexico or Europe.

  • Patagonia

  • Nogales

    Drove through the U.S. side but haven't crossed the border yet.

  • Tucson Gem Show

    Every year, end of January and beginning of February.

    Haven't been yet! People say that town is sort of overrun during Gem Show.

  • Tucson Rodeo

    Late February.

    Haven't been but I wanna. Yeehaw!

  • Guns

    Shoot them! Lots of shooting ranges and shooting-related stuff. Guns are everywhere in the West.

    I've never discharged a firearm. Yet. Look out.

  • Horses

    Ride them!

    Tried it once and they put me on a horse that refused to do anything I wanted.

  • Archery

    Bows and arrows!

    Never tried.

  • Dude Ranches

    These combine horses, guns, archery, campfire food, and bad coffee in a bundled up, tourist-friendly experience.

  • other quirky stuff

    Tucson thinks of itself as weird.

    IMO, Tucson doesn't achieve a California level of weird; I'd put it somewhere around "quirky". But still fun.
